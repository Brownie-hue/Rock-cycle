Introduction

The rock cycle is the continuous process by which rocks are formed, broken down, and transformed from one type to another over time. It explains how igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks change through natural processes such as cooling, weathering, erosion, heat, and pressure.Rocks can go from one type to any other type through different paths: Igneous → Sedimentary: Weathering and erosion break igneous rocks into sediment → deposition, compaction, and cementation → sedimentary rock. Sedimentary → Metamorphic: Buried deep → heat and pressure → metamorphic rock. Metamorphic → Igneous: Melted into magma → cools → igneous rock. Any rock → back to any other: Through melting, weathering, heat/pressure, etc.

It involves igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks, driven by Earth's internal heat and surface processes.
