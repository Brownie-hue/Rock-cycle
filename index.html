Introduction

The rock cycle is the continuous process by which rocks are formed, broken down, and transformed from one type to another over time. It explains how igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks change through natural processes such as cooling, weathering, erosion, heat, and pressure.

It involves igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks, driven by Earth's internal heat and surface processes.
