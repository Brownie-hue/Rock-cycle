About Metamorphic Rocks

WHEN IGNEOUS OR SEDIMENTARY ROCKS ARE BURIED DEEP WITHIN THE EARTH’S CRUST, HEAT AND PRESSURE TRANSFORM THEM INTO A NEW TYPE OF THE ROCK, CALLED METAMORPHIC ROCK.

Metamorphic Rocks – Key Points

  • Formed when already existing rocks are changed by strong heat and/or pressure (but do not melt).
  • Usually made deep inside the Earth or near hot magma.
  • Two kinds: Foliated (have layers or bands) and Non-foliated (no layers).
  • Example of foliated: slate (from shale), schist, gneiss.
  • Example of non-foliated: marble (from limestone), quartzite (from sandstone).
  • Harder and more compact than the original rock; minerals often recrystallize.

Examples: Marble, Slate.
