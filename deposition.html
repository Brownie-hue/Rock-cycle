Deposition & Compaction

Processes

Deposition (also called sedimentation) happens when the moving forces—like rivers, wind, glaciers, or gravity—slow down and can no longer carry the sediment. The particles settle out and drop to the bottom, building up in layers. This often occurs in calm places like ocean floors, lake bottoms, river deltas, or floodplains. Over time, these layers pile up, forming thick deposits of sand, mud, gravel, or shells. Compaction follows as more and more sediment layers build on top. The heavy weight of the upper layers squeezes (compacts) the lower ones, pushing out water and air between the particles and making them pack tightly together. This reduces the space and starts turning the soft, loose sediment into firmer material.

Leads to sedimentary rocks.
